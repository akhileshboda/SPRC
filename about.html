Our Story

About Kindred

A community-centered platform built to bridge the gap between individuals with special needs and the resources, opportunities, and people who can support them.

Mission

Why Kindred exists

Individuals with special needs and their families often navigate a fragmented landscape of programs, providers, and opportunities — with no central point of access. Kindred changes that by bringing everything into one coordinated platform, managed by trained coordinators who know the community.

"Kindred exists to bridge the gap between individuals with special needs and the resources their community offers — connecting participants, families, volunteers, and organizations through a single, trusted platform."
— Kindred Program Mission Statement
Our Approach

What guides us

Three principles shape every decision we make about how the platform is designed and how coordinators serve the community.

Person-Centered

Every participant is a whole person, not a diagnosis. Profiles capture individual interests, goals, and accessibility needs so that opportunities are matched to the person — not the other way around.

Coordinated Access

A trained coordinator manages onboarding, account creation, and matching. This ensures families are supported throughout — not left to navigate the system alone — and that every connection is verified and appropriate.

Community-Driven

Kindred's events and opportunities come from local organizations, employers, and volunteers who are part of the same community as the participants they serve. Every listing is published by a coordinator who vets it first.

Who We Serve

Everyone in the circle of support

Kindred is built for four groups of people — all of whom play a role in the life of a participant.

Participants

Individuals with special needs who are connected to the platform by their guardians and coordinators. Participants can browse events and job opportunities, save interests, and view their weekly newsletter through a personal portal.

Guardians & Families

Parents, legal guardians, and family members who manage participant profiles, document accessibility and medical needs, and approve or reject event and job interest requests on behalf of their participants.

Volunteers

Community members who lend their time and skills to support events and programs. Volunteers register their availability and interests, complete background check clearance, and receive assignments through their portal.

Organizations & Employers

Community organizations, nonprofits, and employers who partner with Kindred to post events, vocational programs, and employment opportunities. Coordinators verify and publish all listings on their behalf.

Getting Started

How to join Kindred

Everything starts with the Sign Up form. Submit your application and a Kindred coordinator will take it from there — reviewing your submission, reaching out, and guiding your family through onboarding at every step.

Start Your Application
1

Submit an application — takes about 5 minutes

Click Sign Up in the navigation bar and fill out the short application form. Tell us who you are, your role (participant, guardian, or volunteer), and a bit about what you're looking for. No account or login is needed to apply.

2

A coordinator reviews your application

Your submission goes directly to a Kindred coordinator. They'll review it and reach out within a few business days to discuss next steps, answer questions, and confirm whether Kindred is the right fit for your family.

3

Your coordinator creates your account

Once enrolled, the coordinator sets up login credentials for you and any linked participants, then sends them to you directly. There is no public self-registration — your account is created and verified by Kindred staff before you ever sign in.

4

Sign in and build your profile

Use the credentials your coordinator provided to log in for the first time. Guardians complete the participant profile — documenting accessibility needs, sensory preferences, interests, and vocational goals — so Kindred can surface the most relevant opportunities.

5

Explore events and jobs; guardians approve

Browse the verified events and job board, flag anything you're interested in, and the guardian approves it. Coordinators track all activity through a work queue and follow up as needed.

6

Stay connected through your portal

Your personal portal keeps everything in one place — a weekly newsletter of new opportunities, notifications from coordinators, and a record of your interests and past activity. Check in whenever you're ready.
